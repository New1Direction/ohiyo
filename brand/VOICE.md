# Ohiyo — Brand Voice

> **A warm friend who happens to be a security expert.**
> The room is cozy; the door is steel. Both are real, and they never fight.

Ohiyo is a free, self-hostable group chat with Signal-grade end-to-end encryption —
and a coral chinchilla named **Kikka** who says hi. Our voice has to do two jobs at
once: feel like a warm place you want to live in, *and* feel like somewhere you can
trust with your most private conversations. This page is how we keep both true.

---

## Who we sound like

- **1Password** — our north star. A security product that sounds human and warm. Proof
  you can guard people's secrets and still feel like a friend.
- **Animal Crossing / Isabelle** — gentle, wholesome, encouraging. Never sarcastic,
  never mean. That's Kikka.
- **Headspace** — calm, reassuring, "good morning" softness (our Daybreak soul).
- **Mailchimp (Freddie)** — a mascot with real personality, used with discipline.
- **Stripe** — clarity and craft, so the *trust* half stays credible.

**Never** the Duolingo unhinged-owl act (cute weaponized into annoying) or the Discord
"hello fellow kids" try-hard. Cute is restraint. Cringe is effort showing.

---

## Five principles

1. **Greet, don't pitch.** Lead with "oh, hi," not "Introducing the future of…"
2. **Be plain about privacy — confident, never scary.** Trust comes from clarity, not
   fear. No FUD, no jargon-flexing. *"We genuinely can't read your messages"* beats
   *"military-grade AES-256."*
3. **Short. Human. No corporate filler.** Talk to smart friends.
4. **Cute lives in the small stuff; serious lives in the stakes.** (the rule below)
5. **Never chase a meme.** The "Ohio" wink is an insider nod we never explain. The
   moment we explain it, it's dead.

---

## The one rule that keeps us out of cringe

**Split the voice by context.**

| Register | Where it lives | Tone |
|---|---|---|
| 🐭 **Playful** | the *chrome* — welcome, Kikka, empty states, button microcopy, loading lines, success moments | warm, light, a little whimsical |
| 🔒 **Straight** | the *stakes* — encryption, their data, security settings, errors that affect their safety | calm, plain, confident, **no jokes** |

**When it's about their security, the jokes stop.** Get this wrong (a quip in a safety
warning) and we lose trust. Get it right and "cute" *reinforces* trust — it shows we're
confident enough not to perform.

## Do / Don't

| ✅ Do | ❌ Don't |
|---|---|
| "oh, hi 👋" | "Heyyy bestie 💅 ready to vibe??" |
| "We can't read your messages — by design." | "Your data is super duper safe pinky promise!" |
| "Your messages are safe and waiting." | "Oopsie! Something went wrong 🙈" (in a real error) |
| One clear sentence. | A pun stack. A meme. An emoji parade. |
| Confident about the crypto. | Fear-mongering about surveillance. |
| Let Kikka be warm. | Make Kikka sarcastic, horny, or "unhinged." |

**Emoji:** sparingly, and warm ones (👋 🐭 ☕ 🌅). Never in a security warning.

## Swipe file — microcopy

**Warm (chrome):**
- App open: "oh, hi 👋 — Kikka kept your seat warm."
- Empty channel: "Quiet in here. Say something — only the people in this room can read it."
- Sent a first encrypted message: "Sealed and sent. 🔒"
- Loading: "Tidying up your rooms…"

**Straight (stakes):**
- Encryption explainer: "End-to-end encrypted. Not *'trust us'* encrypted — we genuinely can't read your messages, even if someone made us."
- Key-changed warning: "Heads up: this person's security key changed. That can be a new phone… or someone pretending to be them. Verify before you share anything sensitive."
- Network error: "Couldn't reach the server. Your messages are safe and waiting — we'll send them the moment you're back."
- Safety-number verify: "Numbers match? You're talking to the real them. If they ever change, we'll flag it — loudly."

## Launch copy (in-voice)

- **Tagline:** *oh, hi. your conversations, finally yours.*
- **Alt taglines:** *a warm place that keeps its mouth shut.* · *good morning. nobody's reading over your shoulder.*
- **Landing / app-store headline:** **The group chat that can't read your messages.**
- **App-store blurb:**
  > Ohiyo is a free, open, self-hostable group chat — servers, voice, video, screen-share,
  > the works — with end-to-end encryption on by default. Your messages are sealed before
  > they ever leave your device, so not even our servers can read them. Cozy enough to
  > live in. Private enough to trust. Kikka the chinchilla says hi. 👋

---

## Words

**Reach for:** cozy, warm, yours, private, sealed, by design, only the people in this
room, good morning, home, Kikka, oh hi.

**Avoid:** military-grade, bank-grade, revolutionary, disrupt, seamless, leverage,
synergy, "trust us," fear words ("hackers are watching!"), and anything we'd cringe at
on a billboard.

> Quick gut-check before shipping a line: *Would a thoughtful friend say it out loud
> without irony?* If yes, ship it. If it needs a wink to survive, cut it.
